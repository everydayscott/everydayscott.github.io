About Me

My name is Scott Johnson. I was born in California and mostly grew up in Tennessee. I've lived in Louisville KY and Seattle WA, each for a time, but now I'm back home in Middle Tennessee. I don't plan on leaving the Nashville area again; this is where I met my wife, it's where our families are settled, it's where we built a house, and it's where we want to raise out kids.

I am a Site Reliability Engineer for Deloitte. I'm an individual contributor in a technical role that requires skills to configure and optimize web front-ends, database back-ends, and load balancers. We are organized into DevOps teams, so I've become a subject matter expert on a small number of applications, doing reliability improvements, creating automation, managing build and release pipelines, and doing change management. I spend a lot of time writing PowerShell. Previously I worked in an Application Engineering role for Take Care Health (now called Premise Health), which was owned by Walgreens at the time. It was a technical analyst role working with medical and pharmacy software. I was responsible for product upgrades, site migrations, and getting support from our various software vendors. I've been doing Application Administration and Systems Analyst work since 2007.

I met my wife, Lauren, in 2010 because a mutual friend set us up on a blind date. We got married in 2011 and went to Walt Disney World on our honeymoon. We got two dogs, miniature schauzers, then had two kids, both girls. Now we both drive family cars and take the kids to ballet lessons, soccer games, and occassionally Walt Disney World, too.
